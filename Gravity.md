### Entropy as the Mechanism of Deconstruction
Entropy in the framework is **not a thermodynamic add-on** but the direct count of **logical information residing outside an observer’s local light-cone** (or holographic screen). It quantifies the unresolved, alternating distinctions in the zero-free-action histories that the observer cannot access.

- The full zero-action completeness is **deconstructed** by **tracing out** the inaccessible paths.
- This produces an effective mixed state \(\rho\) (averaged over sampled zero-action histories).
- The von Neumann entropy
  \[
  S = -\operatorname{Tr}(\rho \ln \rho)

This tracing-out is the literal *deconstruction*: the complete, symmetric network of balanced distinctions (which would otherwise remain a flat relational manifold with no preferred geometry) is partially discarded. What remains is a single consistent “stationary” slicing—the observer’s classical history.

   \[
   S = \frac{A}{4\ell_P^2}

Gravity therefore appears as the geometric back-reaction to information hiding: the effective least-action paths (geodesics) bend precisely because the full zero-action manifold has been deconstructed into an observer-centric slice screened by an area-proportional entropy reservoir.
