# Beta Decay and the Majorana Neutrino

In the Quantum Logical Framework (QLF), nuclear decay is not a random probabilistic event; it is the deterministic restructuring of a topological Markov Blanket to relieve logical stress.

## 1. The Majorana Neutrino as Non-Chiral Logic
A Majorana fermion is a particle that is its own antiparticle. In a universe governed by discrete topological sequences, a particle's "anti" state is simply its Hermitian conjugate (its geometric inverse).
* Chiral particles (like electrons) have distinct anti-states because their loops have handedness (e.g., Left-Handed `^<v>` vs. Right-Handed `^>v<`).
* A **Majorana Neutrino** is computationally modeled as a **Non-Chiral ZFA Loop**. It achieves Zero Free Action through pure linear oscillation (e.g., `^v` or `<>`). Because its geometry is perfectly symmetric, its conjugate is identical to itself. It is a purely tautological logical structure.

A Hadron (like a Neutron) is a Markov Blanket—a macroscopic Context that hides complex fractional logic (quarks) from the surrounding vacuum Environment. 

A free Neutron contains a topological stress (excess Bound Action) relative to the vacuum. (The Proton is a net-charge *deficit*, not a free observable: it is completed into a neutral hydrogen-class closure by a negative lepton — and the β-decay event co-produces exactly that completer, the electron below. Which lepton variety completes it — `e⁻`/`μ⁻`/`τ⁻` → hydrogen / muonic / tauonic — is a generation degree of freedom; see [Weak_Force.md](Weak_Force.md) §4a.)
* **The Unspooling:** The Neutron opens its topological boundary, unspooling the excess logic.

  1. **The Electron:** A highly chiral, tightly bound ZFA loop that carries away the asymmetric logical debt.
