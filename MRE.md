# MRE.md: Maximal Relative Entropy via 1/2-Spin ZFA Closures

**Maximal Relative Entropy in the Quantum Logical Framework (QLF)** *1/2-spin Zero Free Action (ZFA) as the entropy-maximizing logical atom of constructive possibilist synthesis*

## Abstract

In the QLF, every physical event is a **ZFA closure** — a topological pruning of the possibility tree that leaves zero unresolved free action. The **half-spin (spin-1/2) ZFA** is the *minimal, indivisible* such closure. This document formalizes the intuition that a 1/2-spin ZFA achieves **maximum relative entropy** (Kullback–Leibler divergence) between the pre-closure prior (the full unresolved possibility tree) and the post-closure posterior.  

This maximal information gain per logical fold is why 1/2-spin is the foundational building block of fermions, fluxoids, and the Zeno-pruned Ruliad in QuantumOS. It unifies the topological embedding of spin-1/2, the observer-relative nature of entropy, and the Hermitian conjugacy required by ZFA.

## 1. Intuition

- Before closure, a history string in the [8-axis twist alphabet](./eight-twists-sufficiency.md) encodes an **exponentially branching possibility tree**.
- A 1/2-spin ZFA loop (one fluxoid, one orthogonal 4-twist cycle `^<v>` or equivalent) forces an **exact Hermitian conjugate** and halves the future space exactly once.
- Any coarser split (higher spin, non-binary pruning) leaves residual free action unresolved $\rightarrow$ lower relative entropy per event.
- Thus the 1/2-spin closure extracts the *largest possible surprise* (information gain) while satisfying the Zero Free Action constraint.

This matches the standard quantum fact that a qubit in the maximally mixed state has the highest von Neumann entropy $S = \ln 2$ of any two-level system — here made fully constructive and causal.

## 2. Formalization

### 2.1 Possibility Tree and Relative Entropy

Let $\mathcal{T}$ be the pre-closure possibility tree generated by unresolved twists.  
The prior $p$ is uniform over all leaves of $\mathcal{T}$.  
A 1/2-spin ZFA closure defines a binary partition $$\mathcal{T} = \mathcal{T}_{+} \cup \mathcal{T}_{-}$$(Hermitian pair) and projects onto the posterior$$q = \delta_{\text{closed branch}}$$  
(i.e., a Kronecker delta on the resolved history).

The **relative entropy** (KL divergence) realized by this closure is
$$D_{\text{KL}}(p \| q) = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{T}} p(x) \ln \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} = \ln 2$$
exactly when the partition is 50/50. Any other split yields strictly lower $D_{\text{KL}}$.

In QuCalc notation (see [QuCalc.md](./QuCalc.md) and [BraKetRhoQuCalc.md](./BraKetRhoQuCalc.md)):
$$\text{ZFA}_{1/2} \equiv \text{fluxoid}(\, ^\smallfrown\!\! < \!\! \smallsmile\!\! v \, ) \quad \mapsto \quad \rho \to \frac{1}{2} I$$
where the reduced density matrix $\rho$ after the minimal loop is maximally mixed, maximizing $S(\rho) = -\text{Tr}(\rho \ln \rho) = \ln 2$.
